What If the Roman Empire Never Fell?

In 476 AD, the Western Roman Empire collapsed. But what if it didn't? Without Rome's fall, there's no Dark Ages. Latin becomes the global language. The scientific revolution starts 700 years early. The Americas are colonized by Rome in 900 AD.

The Man Who Survived Both Atomic Bombs

Tsutomu Yamaguchi was in Hiroshima on August 6, 1945, when the first atomic bomb fell. He survived.

He then took a train back to his hometown — Nagasaki. Three days later, the second atomic bomb fell. He survived again.

Yamaguchi is the only person officially recognized by the Japanese government as having survived both atomic bombings. He lived to be 93 years old.

When asked how he survived, he said: "I was just lucky, I guess."

What If the Library of Alexandria Never Burned?

The Great Library of Alexandria was the ancient world's greatest repository of knowledge. When it burned, humanity lost countless works of science, philosophy, and literature. But what if it had survived?

With the Library intact, the Scientific Revolution might have happened a thousand years early. Greek mathematics, Egyptian medicine, and Babylonian astronomy would have been preserved and built upon.

We might have landed on the moon in the year 1000. We might have cured diseases that killed millions. We might have avoided the Dark Ages entirely.

All because one building didn't burn down.

The Shortest War in History Lasted 38 Minutes

On August 27, 1896, Britain and Zanzibar went to war. It lasted 38 minutes.

The conflict began when the Sultan of Zanzibar died and his nephew seized power without British approval. The British demanded he step down. He refused.

At 9:00 AM, British warships opened fire on the palace. By 9:38 AM, the palace was destroyed, the nephew had fled, and the war was over.

One side lost 500 men. The other lost one.

Rasputin Survived Poison, Stabbing, and Drowning

In 1916, Russian nobles tried to kill the mad monk. They poisoned him with enough cyanide to kill five men. He didn't die. They shot him. Still alive. They stabbed him repeatedly. Finally, they threw him into a frozen river. Only then did he finally die.

The Last Message From the Titanic

When the Titanic sank in 1912, the ship's band kept playing as the vessel went under. The bandleader, Wallace Hartley, wrote one final message and put it in a bottle. Three days later, it washed up in Ireland.

Australia Lost a War to Birds

In 1932, the Australian military declared war on 20,000 emus that were destroying crops. The army deployed soldiers with machine guns. The emus won. After a week, only 50 emus had been killed. Australia surrendered.

The Dancing Plague That Killed Hundreds

In 1518, a woman in Strasbourg began dancing in the street. Within a month, 400 people had joined her. They couldn't stop. Some danced until their hearts gave out. The cause was never found. To this day, it remains one of history's strangest mysteries.

What If Hitler Got Into Art School?

In 1907, Adolf Hitler applied to the Vienna Academy of Fine Arts. He was rejected. Twice. What if they said yes? He becomes a painter. Not a dictator. No Nazi party. No World War II. Sixty million lives could have been saved.
